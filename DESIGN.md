# MathPath Tutor Design Direction

## Archetype

Adaptive learning companion.

MathPath Tutor should feel encouraging, focused, capable, and age-flexible. It should work for students from grade school through high school without feeling either childish or corporate.

## User Feeling

Students should feel capable. Parents and teachers should feel progress is visible and trustworthy.

## Visual Direction

- Guided lesson paths
- Progress maps
- Practice cards
- Mistake review
- Warm achievement states
- Parent and teacher summary views

## Token Direction

- Radius: 12px for learning surfaces, 8px for controls
- Density: loose
- Typography: rounded readable sans
- Palette: bright but balanced, with multiple subject accents
- Icons: friendly line/filled mix
- Motion: encouraging microinteractions

## Avoid

- Infantilized visuals
- Corporate dashboard density
- Overly gamified reward spam
- Monochrome school-blue palette
- Generic edtech templates

## AI Prompt Recipe

```text
Design an adaptive math tutoring platform for students from grade school through high school. Use warm progress paths, readable lesson cards, mistake review, and encouraging microinteractions. Make it friendly but not childish.
```

## QA Checklist

- Does the UI make learning feel approachable without becoming childish?
- Are mistakes framed as progress and review?
- Can parents or teachers quickly understand growth?
- Does it avoid looking like a corporate admin dashboard?
